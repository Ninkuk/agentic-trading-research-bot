# Constellation Software (TSX:CSU) — 2026-07-10

## 1. Verdict and thesis

Constellation is one of the best capital-allocation machines in public
markets — a portfolio of sticky, mission-critical vertical-market-software
(VMS) cash cows whose ~$2.7B/yr of operating cash is redeployed at ~20% ROIC
into more of the same — and, unusually for a serial acquirer, the price is
**not** a nosebleed on cash economics (~15× P/FCF, 6.6% FCF yield; the 56× GAAP
PE is an accounting artifact). But everything that would make it a *great* buy
rather than a merely fair one hinges on a reinvestment runway that CSU has
**deliberately stopped disclosing** — so the single number the thesis rests on
can no longer be monitored.

**Kill-thesis verdict: UNPROVEN.** 6 load-bearing conditions; 3 survive attack
(historical ROIC visible in the cash flows, stable FCF conversion, conservative
leverage), 3 are unverifiable-by-disclosure (forward incremental ROIC,
reinvestment runway at scale, culture continuity post-Leonard). Nothing is
refuted; the evidence to prove the upside case does not exist. The closest
attack to landing: the law-of-large-numbers runway question is now
*un-monitorable* because CSU reduced deal-level disclosure — not an accident of
research, a choice by the company.

## 2. Business — created / captured / protected

**Created.** Each subsidiary sells software that runs a niche industry's core
operations — the kind of vertical too small to interest a large horizontal
vendor (transit ticketing, credit unions, funeral homes, golf clubs, agri-co-ops,
public-sector permitting). The customer gets a system that is a *small* line
item but *mission-critical*: the cost of it failing dwarfs its price. That
asymmetry is the source of both pricing power and retention.

**Captured.** Three concrete mechanisms, not "software revenue":
1. **Recurring maintenance + SaaS** — high-margin, sticky, prepaid (deferred
   revenue is negative working capital: −$804M CAD, i.e. customers fund the
   business). Low-single-digit attrition.
2. **Above-inflation price increases** on locked-in installed bases.
3. **Capital allocation itself** — the scarce asset. Cash from (1)+(2) is
   redeployed into acquiring more VMS businesses at ~1× revenue / mid-single-digit
   × cash flow, earning ~20% cash-on-cash. This is the real engine; organic
   growth is low-single-digit.

**Protected.**
- *Unit level:* switching costs (data/workflow/regulatory entanglement) in
  verticals whose total addressable market is too small to attract a
  well-capitalized entrant. Thousands of such products → no single disruption
  is fatal.
- *Holding level (the durable moat):* reputation as the *permanent* home for
  founder-owned VMS businesses (CSU rarely resells or guts them), which buys
  proprietary, non-auctioned deal flow; plus a decentralized structure with
  central hurdle-rate discipline. The scarce, hard-to-copy asset is the
  *discipline to say no* — a culture, not a patent.
- Mechanism test (not a checkbox): what stops a competitor tomorrow? At the
  unit level, the sub-scale TAM. At the holding level, a 25-year reputation and
  deal network a new entrant cannot buy. **The honest gap: nothing stops the
  *reinvestment rate* from decaying as CSU outgrows the pool of cheap targets —
  that silence is condition 2 below.**

## 3. Threads pulled (including dead ends)

**Load-bearing:**
- **The FCF double-count (biggest thread, and a mid-analysis self-correction).**
  Reported FCF (`ncfo + capex` = $2.73B USD TTM) *excludes* the ~$1.3–1.9B/yr of
  acquisitions that fund growth (booked in *investing* CF as
  `payments_for_business_acquisitions`: −$1.93B TTM, −$1.34B FY25, −$1.52B FY24).
  You cannot count full FCF *and* acquisition-driven growth — that double-counts.
  Resolved in §4: valued cleanly, implied return is ~12–16%, not the naive
  14–20%.
- **PE 56 vs P/FCF 15.** Net income is only ~$0.75B USD vs $2.73B FCF — a 3.6×
  gap. Cause: amortization of acquired intangibles (non-cash) + fair-value
  adjustments and minority interests on the consolidated-but-not-wholly-owned
  subsidiaries (Topicus, Lumine, TSS). FCF is the truer earnings metric here;
  GAAP PE is misleading. Confirmed: the gap is structural and recurring.
- **The machine actually works — checked against the cash, not management.**
  2021→2025 they deployed ~$7.3B USD cumulative on acquisitions; revenue rose
  ~$6.5B (5.11B→11.62B) → ~0.9× revenue paid, which at ~22% FCF margin implies
  ~19% cash-on-cash. The ~20% ROIC is visible directly in the statements, not
  just asserted. **But this is backward-looking.**

**Dead ends (work done, ruled out):**
- *Leverage* — net debt/EBITDA ~1× (USD), gross debt/EBITDA 2.17× (CAD),
  interest coverage 7.3×, Altman Z 4.27, F-Score 5. Conservative. Not a kill.
- *Dividend safety* — 0.2% yield, flat ~$1/sh, 11% payout. Irrelevant by design;
  they should retain to reinvest, and do.
- *Balance-sheet distress* — none. Negative working capital is *float*, a
  positive here.
- *Acquisition-spend explosion* — feared runway strain would show as ballooning
  spend; instead absolute spend is fairly flat ($1.2–1.9B), which cuts both ways
  (discipline, or difficulty deploying proportionally at scale).

## 4. Valuation — the implied return *is* the runway bet

Inputs (live, CAD, `/statistics/`): market cap 57.97B, FCF 3.81B, P/FCF 15.2,
FCF yield 6.6%, EV/EBITDA 17.5. Reinvestment rate R ≈ 1.9/2.73 ≈ 0.70; realized
ROIC ≈ 0.20 → implied sustainable growth g = R×ROIC ≈ 14%.

Solving for the annual return the price already implies, the answer depends
**entirely** on how long 20%-ROIC reinvestment persists:

| Scenario | Assumption | Implied return |
|---|---|---|
| **Floor** | They never make another value-add deal; full FCF distributed, ~3–5% organic growth forever | **~9.5–11.5%/yr** |
| **Base** | Reinvest at 20% for ~10–15 yrs, then fade to GDP-like terminal | **~13–15%/yr** |
| **Upside** | 20%-ROIC reinvestment persists indefinitely (Gordon: k = payout/PFCF + g) | **~16%/yr** |

(Reverse-DCF and a reinvestment-Gordon frame agree on this bracket. The naive
"grow full FCF at 14%" read of 14–20% overstates by ignoring reinvestment; an
earlier "grow owner-FCF-after-acquisitions at 14%" read of 9–11% *understates*
by subtracting reinvestment from the base while keeping its growth — both were
wrong, and the corrected bracket is ~11–16%.)

**Read:** at ~15× FCF you are not overpaying for the quality; even the
no-more-good-deals floor clears ~10%. But the difference between a good outcome
(11%) and an excellent one (16%) is the reinvestment runway — the exact thing
that cannot be verified. The market is pricing the base case.

Next earnings **2026-07-23** (13 days out) — not into it here; timing noted.

## 5. Falsifiers (what would make me sell)

- **Incremental ROIC decays** — hurdle-rate commentary drops, or acquired-revenue
  multiples paid rise materially above ~1× (observable in MD&A / shareholder
  letters on SEDAR+).
- **Runway exhaustion** — cash builds up undeployed, or (the mirror image) a
  large *debt-funded* deal at a rich multiple. Either signals the small-tuck-in
  engine is out of road.
- **Organic growth persistently negative net of price** — the base cash cows
  eroding faster than acquisitions replace them.
- **FCF/NI gap widens for non-amortization reasons** — cash taxes rising, or the
  deferred-revenue float reversing.
- **Culture drift** — departures of key operating-group heads; further reduction
  in disclosure; a shift away from the buy-shares-and-hold comp structure.

A thesis with these falsifiers is an argument, not just a position — good.

## 6. UNKNOWNs

- **Forward incremental deployed-capital IRR at current scale.** The single
  load-bearing number. Historical ~20% is visible; forward is not — CSU has
  *reduced* deal-level disclosure (Leonard's letters shrank). Source would be
  SEDAR+ MD&A / shareholder letters. **Absence does not kill the thesis but
  demotes it to UNPROVEN** — it is the crux of the base-vs-upside return.
- **Organic growth net of price and FX.** Needed for the valuation floor; not
  available from live data. Source: interim/annual MD&A constant-currency
  organic-growth disclosure (SEDAR+). Not pulled (CSU files on SEDAR+, not SEC;
  outside this repo's `edgar`/`fundamentals` coverage).
- **AI as threat vs. tailwind to niche VMS** over a 10–15yr horizon. Genuinely
  unassessable. Bounded: revenue is spread across thousands of tiny,
  data-entangled products, so wholesale disruption is slow and piecemeal, and AI
  is also a tuck-in/cost lever for CSU — but "no disruption over the runway" is
  precisely the horizon the upside case needs. UNKNOWN; does not kill near-term.
- **Culture continuity post-Leonard.** Succession to a decentralized system is
  the explicit design, but central capital-allocation discipline is the scarce
  asset and its durability is unverifiable. UNKNOWN.
- **FX for a CAD-based holder** — reports USD, trades CAD; realized return
  includes USD/CAD. Not a kill, noted.
