## What happened

**Plan 001's step-1 spike did its job and stopped the plan** — then found
something bigger. Probing whether stockanalysis's `/history/` endpoint returns
split-adjusted closes revealed that the *existing* harvester was reading the
wrong column entirely. `metrics.close` is stockanalysis's **Previous Close**;
`metrics.price` is the close for `priceDate`. Every row in the permanent,
never-pruned `scorer.db.prices` ledger held a real close stamped with the **next**
trading day's date, which handed `entry_for()` the composite date's own close —
reintroducing the exact overnight look-ahead that function exists to prevent.

That became **plan 000**, which did not exist when this index was written. It was
implemented, adversarially reviewed (VERDICT: SOUND; the core claim was verified
against CBOE's independent feed, 4/4 vs 0/4), and the ledger was rebuilt. Fixing
it also surfaced a second bug: `price` is only trustworthy once the session has
*settled*, so the harvester now takes a `priceDate` only from a snapshot captured
on a strictly later Phoenix day.

**Plan 001 remains BLOCKED**, deliberately. Its premise — that backfilled history
can be written into the existing ledger — was only made *safe* by plan 000. The
backfill itself is now unblocked in principle (`/history/`'s `c` equals
`metrics.price` for the same date, verified on SPY/XLE), but it was never
executed: the plan's STOP fired first, and re-running it should start from a
plan rewritten against the corrected ledger.

**Plan 002 shipped with a critical correction the plan itself missed** — a score-0
signal still widened `ticker_scores.total`, which gates `v_flagged`. Left alone it
would have flagged tickers *because* they report earnings soon. See that plan's
"Execution notes".

## Plan 004: what the reviewer broke

The spec's two load-bearing decisions were wrong, and it had failed to ask the
question that governs both.

1. **The scorer cannot grade an option at all.** `scorer.db.prices` is
   `(symbol, price_date, close)` — equity closes only. There is no option premium
   series anywhere. So `v_decision_outcomes.entry_slippage`
   (`d.fill_price / t.entry_close - 1`) would divide an option **premium** by the
   **underlying's stock close**: a $2.50 premium against AAPL's $307 close reads
   as −99.2% "slippage", written permanently. `v_flag_response` would credit the
   underlying *stock's* forward return to an option decision — a long call that
   was directionally right but bled out on theta scores as a win. The first draft
   called preserving those views "the single most valuable property of the
   design"; it is the mechanism that silently reinterprets an option as its
   underlying. The spec now opens with **Q0**, which gates the whole migration.

2. **`abs(delta)` heat inverts a protective put.** 100 shares + one long put
   (delta −0.5) reports `450` heat where the true one-ATR-day loss is `150` — a
   3× overstatement *at the moment the book got safer*, because `v_group_heat`
   sums per-leg heat with no netting. The spec now requires **signed**
   delta-dollars netted within a group.

Also confirmed and folded in: `position_effect` (open/close) must be ingested —
a sell-to-close of a long call is an exit, not a bearish open, and `side` alone
cannot tell them apart; multi-leg spreads need a `strategy_ref` or they
double-count one bet; expiration/assignment produce no closing fill and were
entirely unmodeled; and `v_unusual_activity` is **not** "ready day one" —
24.5% of rows have `open_interest = 0`, where `vol_oi_ratio` stores raw volume
(`AMZN…P00265000`: volume 8312, OI 0, ratio 8312.0), so its `>= 1.0` filter is
satisfied by every zero-OI contract with any volume.

The reviewer's one overstatement, corrected on verification: it called
`v_unusual_activity` "dominated by" OI=0 rows. Measured, it is 243 of 3,829
(6.3%). Still a real defect, smaller than claimed.

## Backtest look-ahead + baseline fix (2026-07-09, unplanned)

Found while reading the `eia_*` results plan 005 unlocked.

**1. Look-ahead.** `market_obs.obs_date` stored the date a value *describes*, not
the date it was *published*. EIA's crude report covers the week ending Friday but
is released the following Wednesday; `v_pit_market` served it from the Friday, so
the replay traded on a number nobody had for 5 days. Added
`publication_lag_days` to `MARKET_OBS_SIGNALS` (crude 6, natgas 7, `tsy_tga` 1 —
DTS publishes the next business day; exchange feeds 0), applied in
`insert_market_obs`, which now DELETEs the signal first because shifting a date
mints a new `(signal_id, obs_date)` key and stale un-shifted rows would survive
`INSERT OR REPLACE` forever. Deliberately over-lagged by a day so a
holiday-shifted release can never leak.

**2. The bigger one: `hit_rate` had no baseline.** It was compared against 0.5.
SP500 rose **68.5%** of 21-day windows in the sample and XLE **56%**, so any
bullish flag "won" ~0.69 by doing nothing and any bearish flag "lost". `tsy_tga`
bullish printed `hit 0.705, reliable`; its excess over drift is **+0.020**.

Added `v_benchmark_baseline` (unconditional p_up/p_down per benchmark × horizon)
and three columns to `v_replay_efficacy`: `baseline`, `excess`, `beats_baseline`
(the benchmark's drift lies outside the flag's Wilson interval). `reliable` was
left alone — it is a sample-size floor and `advisor/fetch.read_reliable_signals`
depends on that meaning.

**Result: of 48 graded rows, `reliable = 1` for all 48; only 11 beat baseline.**

Surviving after baseline correction (nothing was reweighted — that stays a human
decision per CLAUDE.md):

|---|---|---|---|
| `eia_natgas_storage` | both | +0.03 … +0.08 | consistent, n≈1100–1600 |

## Two structural fixes (2026-07-09)

Both are mechanism, not inference. Neither changes a signal weight.

**1. Split the shared EIA score constant.** `eia_crude_stocks` and
`eia_natgas_storage` interpolated the same `EIA_WEEKLY_CHANGE_SCORE`, so neither
could be retuned without silently retuning the other — two different commodities
on two different weekly release schedules. Now `EIA_CRUDE_CHANGE_SCORE` and
`EIA_NATGAS_CHANGE_SCORE`, identical today, independently tunable. Pinned by an
**AST-based** test: Python interns equal string literals, so `is not` cannot
express "two independent assignments" — the invariant is syntactic. Both
re-merge mutations (aliasing in composite; repointing in backtest) fail the tests.

**2. Grade one observation per report, not per forward-filled day.**
`v_pit_market` forward-fills a weekly EIA value across ~5 trading days;
`v_replay_efficacy` counted each of those as an independent measurement. `n=339`
was 70 reports. Every Wilson interval was ~√5 too narrow.

`source_date` (the observation actually being served) now threads through
`v_pit_signal` / `v_pit_market` / `v_pit_pcr` → `v_replay_flags` →
`v_replay_efficacy`, which dedupes to the first as-of date per observation.
`n_obs` is the honest count; `n_days` is kept for reference. The Wilson CI and
the `reliable` floor are computed on observations.

**What that did to the conclusions:**

| | before | after |
|---|---|---|
| rows flagged `beats_baseline` | 11 | **6** |
| `eia_crude_stocks` bearish "anti-signal" | flagged at 10d and 21d | **not flagged at any horizon** |
| `eia_natgas_storage` | 6 of 6 arms flagged | **3 of 6** |

The crude "anti-signal" I reported to the user was an artifact of counting 70
weekly reports as 339 daily observations. It is gone.

Six rows still clear a *nominal* 95% bar, and the run now prints that they are
uncorrected across 48 comparisons (~2 expected by chance) and that overlapping
forward windows make even the observation count optimistic. Under Bonferroni,
`fred_hy_spread bearish` (n=53) does not survive. **No weight was changed.**

A self-check worth recording: the view reports 70 crude observations where a
hand query said 67. The view is right — 47 of 1,438 report dates fall on market
holidays, and the hand query dropped them instead of grading at the next close.

## Findings considered and rejected

- **"The efficacy→reweight loop has no report tooling."** *False.*
  `deploy/launchd/dashboard.py:547` already renders `v_signal_recommendation`, and
  `sources/combiners/advisor/fetch.py` consumes `v_signal_efficacy`. The residual
  gap is only that nothing *diffs* verdicts against the live weights in
  `composite/catalog.py` — too small to plan standalone.
- **Deleting the ~78 "unconsumed" `v_*` views.** Overstated. Every view is
  referenced by its own tests; they are unconsumed by the *opinion pipeline*, not
  dead. Four whole DBs (`options`, `earnings`, `ats`, `sec_fundamentals`) feed
  nothing but a staleness check — leverage is in consuming the good ones, not
  deleting the rest. `earnings.db` is now consumed (plan 002).
- **Wiring `ats.db` (dark pools) or `sec_fundamentals.db` (valuation) into
  composite.** Real asymmetries, but each needs a scoring thesis that does not
  exist, and the short/squeeze family already double-counts one phenomenon
  (`composite/catalog.py`). Adding ungraded signals before grading is possible is
  the wrong order.
- **CFTC per-market grain.** Genuine information loss by aggregation and the
  richest remaining macro idea, but `docs/BACKLOG.md` already scopes the
  point-in-time replay it depends on, which depends on plan 001's benchmark
  backfill. Sequence: 001 → CFTC replay → per-market grain.
- **A `user_version` schema-migration framework.** The ad-hoc
  `PRAGMA table_info` + `ALTER` idiom works, is tested, and is touched rarely.
  Not worth formalizing yet.
- **A `--json` / `--all` / `--dry-run` dispatcher surface.** Real DX gap, but DX,
  not direction. Worth a separate `improve dx` pass.
